CssSelector locating strategies

1. ID:
	tagname#<id value>
	e.g.: input#input-email
	
	#<id value>
	e.g.: #input-email
	
	tagname[id='<id value>'] - not recommanded
	e.g.: input[id='input-email']
	
2. Class:
	tagname.<class value>
	e.g.: button.orangehrm-login-button
	
	.<class value>
	e.g.: .orangehrm-login-button
	
	tagname[class='<class value>'] - not recommanded
	e.g.: button[class='orangehrm-login-button']
	
3. Attribute:
	tagname[<attribute>='<attribute value>']
	e.g.: input[placeholder='Username']
	
4. Combining attributes:

	4.1 ID and attribute:
		tagname#<id value>[<attribute>='<attribute value>']
		e.g.: input#input-email[placeholder='E-Mail Address']
		
	4.2 Class and attribute:
		tagname.<class value>[<attribute>='<attribute value>']
		e.g.: input.btn-primary[value='Login']
	
	4.3 ID, Class and attribute:
		tagname#<id value>.<class value>[<attribute>='<attribute value>']
		e.g.: input#input-firstname.form-control[placeholder='First Name']
	
5. Substring selector
	5.1 * Matching substring (contains)
		tagname[<attribute>*='<Substring>']
		e.g.: input[name^='first']
		
	5.1 ^ Matching prefix (starts with)
		tagname[<attribute>^='<prefix string>']
		e.g.: input[placeholder^='Last']
		
	5.3 $ Matching suffix (ends with)
		tagname[<attribute>$='<suffix string>']
		e.g.: a[href$='/forgotten']
		
6. Parent Child hierarchy
	6.1 Descendant: Adding space between two or more tagnames, identifies direct and in direct child.
		tagname tagname
		e.g.: aside a[href$='/logout']
		
	6.2 Direct child: Adding > between two tagnames, identifies direct child.
		tagname>tagname
		e.g.: ul.oxd-dropdown-menu>li
		
	6.3 Tagname with :nth-child(index): Locate the child element from the list using the index number.
		tagname:nth-child(index)
		e.g.: ul.oxd-dropdown-menu li:nth-child(4)
